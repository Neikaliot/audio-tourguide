Explore Tây Hồ District: A Journey Through Hanoi's Scenic and Culinary Highlights

Tây Hồ district, one of Hanoi's most vibrant areas, offers stunning lake views, rich history, and an array of delicious street food. It’s a perfect blend of culture, nature, and local cuisine that invites you to discover the best of what Hanoi has to offer.

Phở Cuốn Ngũ Xã

Phở Cuốn Ngũ Xã is a must-visit destination for a fresh take on Hanoi's traditional phở. Instead of the familiar noodle soup, you’ll be treated to phở cuốn—soft rice noodle rolls filled with seasoned beef and fresh herbs. These rolls are light yet satisfying, and their refreshing taste makes them the perfect snack or meal as you explore Tây Hồ district. The restaurant is beloved by locals and visitors alike for its simple yet flavorful twist on a classic dish.

Bánh Tôm Hồ Tây

When in Tây Hồ, make sure to try Bánh Tôm Hồ Tây, the district’s famed crispy shrimp cakes. This delightful dish consists of freshly caught shrimp, fried in a crispy batter, and served hot and golden brown. The cakes are crunchy on the outside and tender inside, making them the ideal snack as you stroll along the lake. Pair it with a cool drink, and you'll have the perfect lakeside treat.

Bún Ốc Cô Huệ

For those seeking a more adventurous dish, Bún Ốc Cô Huệ serves up one of Tây Hồ’s most unique specialties: snail noodles. This bowl of bún ốc features tender snails swimming in a rich, flavorful broth that’s both savory and tangy. Fresh herbs and tomatoes add balance, making every bite an explosion of flavors. If you're a fan of Hanoi’s rich culinary traditions, this dish is a must-try.

Chè Tây Hồ

When you need a sweet escape, head to Chè Tây Hồ, a beloved local stall offering a variety of traditional Vietnamese sweet soups. Chè is a refreshing treat made with ingredients like coconut milk, beans, jelly, and fruits. Each bowl is a mix of textures and flavors, perfect for cooling down on a warm day. Whether you prefer a light, fruity version or something richer with coconut and mung beans, this spot will satisfy your dessert cravings.

Bánh Xèo & Nem Lụi

Wrap up your culinary tour of Tây Hồ with a visit to a local favorite: Bánh Xèo & Nem Lụi. Bánh xèo, or crispy Vietnamese pancakes, are made with rice flour and turmeric and filled with pork, shrimp, and bean sprouts. These savory pancakes are served with fresh herbs and dipped in a flavorful fish sauce. Alongside the pancakes, try nem lụi, grilled pork skewers with a hint of lemongrass. Together, they offer a burst of flavors and textures that will leave your taste buds delighted.
